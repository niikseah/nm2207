about this website

I made this website to analyse the data of the top 250 anime of 2023.

Using this data, I hope to analyse viewers' preferences in terms of

  • type of anime watched (TV, movie, etc.)

  • animation studio (art style preference)

  • duration of episodes

  • genre of anime

    • With this analysis, producers are able to better understand the needs and wants of their audience and make content catered to them.

    What does this tell us about consumers' tastes?



    Madhouse took the top spot.
    Their art style is the most popular amongst animation studios.
    Television is the most popular type of animation by a landslide! Anime watchers prefer episodes over whole movies
    Viewers tend to prefer anime with episodes' durations ranging from more than 60 minutes to 90 minutes!
    Episodes over 90 minutes were unpopular or not even common in the first place.
    Drama seems to be the most popular genre in anime, at least for the year of 2023!

    For my final project, I did a data story on the top 250 anime of 2023. This differs from my initial idea of Cafe in a City, a website containing an interactive map that helps Singaporeans in cafe-hopping. This change is due to a lack of accurate data on Singapore's cafes. I eventually chose this topic because I am interested in anime and seeing the data got me curious as to what consumers' preferences were. This will be useful information for both the consumers and the producers. The anime producers can see what consumers' preferences are and make content catered to their wants. The website also shows anime watchers which are the top 10 anime for 2023 and also has a random anime generator for if they do not know what to watch next. For my website, I decided to make it black and white not only because I liked the monochrome aesthetic of it, but also to match the black and white panels of manga that animes usually are adapted from. Data might be boring, so I added pictures here and there to make the website nicer and more interesting, while also not distracting people from the charts. I also added a video at the start to capture attention while also displaying the beauty of anime. I made charts based on different categories that I feel are relevant to producers and consumers of anime: one example being the popularity of different genres in anime. I added buttons to change chart types as well so people can look at the information in different ways. To be honest, the designing of the website engaged me more. I viewed differently websites for inspiration: A24's website and StackedHomes' website mainly. I delved more into CSS than Javascript for my project but I learned more about both tools, as well as HTML. I figured out that I could use a text editor online to help input my data (thankfully) because I had too many data points but was unable to use a fetch() function to import my data. For my final project, I learned a lot of things through trial and error, as well as using Internet sources. Some of these things I did not put into the website as I decided it was not useful for my website, but those that I did put on the website are credited. Personally, it was hard knowing what to do with the charts as the customizations are not taught in class. I had to use the chart.js youtube channel to learn more about them. This goes for CSS as well, I managed to learn a lot more about CSS through the Internet. While I believe that I tried my best, I still believe that maybe the data could be more deeply analyzed. The colours of my chart are chosen deliberately and I do acknowledge that a black-grey colour scheme is not the best at highlighting data but it is my personal design choice. If given more time, I would have hoped to add a button to recommend anime based on the different categories but I decided that for the final project, just one that churns out a random anime from the top 250 is sufficient. Lastly, I would have made the website more interactive, giving information wherever users hover or click. The website is quite one-dimensional and there are not any links to different pages for different features as I would like but for a data story final project, I think it is fine. This was my first time coding and my first time designing a website and I found this useful and fun as well, contrary to what I first expected signing up for this module. [623 words]
